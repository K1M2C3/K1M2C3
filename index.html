Stop the slaughter, free the animals!

Companies have been using animals from the earliest of the 3rd and 4th centuries BCE, all the way to today. Animal testing didn't become a problem until 1938, when the united states food, drug and cosmetic act was signed into law, making it so there is some safety substantiation of cosmetic products.

The animals used in testing are not just rats and guinea pig. They also use animals like mice, fish, rabbits, hamsters, farm animals, birds, cats, dogs, mini-pigs, and non-human primates like monkeys, and in some coutries, chimpanzees.

While rats and guinea pigs make up most of the animals being tested on there are still a lot of other animals being tested on just not as much as them.

Very few country have ban animal testing all together. While most country still allow it and some even say companies have to test there products.

Over 100 million animals are killed because of testing. At first testing on animals was not a huge problem, but when the law that say that all product must be tested before they can be sold was sign companies started to use animals to do the testing.
